# The Last Dream

I have dreamed this dream for as long as I can remember.

Every time it begins the same way.

I stand beneath a sky that does not belong to this world.

Above me, a city floats among the clouds, brighter than the sun, larger than any kingdom ever built by human hands.

And every time, it is falling.

The towers collapse first.

Then the bridges.

Then the great ring in the heavens shatters into countless fragments of light.

The sky burns.

The dragons scream.

And somewhere within the fire, someone calls my name.

Not the name I know.

A different one.

A forgotten one.

A name that feels older than the mountains.

Older than the rivers.

Older than the world itself.

I try to remember it.

I always try.

But before I can reach it, the dream ends.

And I wake.

---

When I opened my eyes that morning, the rain was tapping softly against the roof.

The village was still asleep.

Mist drifted between the houses.

The scent of wet earth filled the air.

For a moment, everything felt normal.

The same mountains.

The same river.

The same life I had always known.

Yet something was different.

I could feel it.

Like a distant storm waiting beyond the horizon.

Like a memory struggling to surface.

Like someone watching from far away.

I stepped outside and looked toward the forest.

The elders often spoke of the ruins hidden beyond those trees.

Ancient places abandoned by gods.

Places no one should disturb.

As a child, I believed their stories.

As I grew older, I laughed at them.

Now, for reasons I could not explain, I could not stop staring at the forest.

It felt as though something inside was calling to me.

Waiting for me.

Remembering me.

---

That was the morning everything changed.

The morning the past found me.

The morning the ruins of Heaven awakened.

And the morning I began to discover that the history of this world was a lie.

Because long before I was born...

Long before kingdoms rose and fell...

Long before dragons became legends...

I had lived another life.

And somehow...

That life was not finished with me yet.
