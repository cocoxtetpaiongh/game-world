# Art Direction

## Core Identity

Age of Legends: Echoes of Heaven is not medieval fantasy.

It is not science fiction.

It is not cyberpunk.

The visual identity is Mythological Future Fantasy.

A world where mythology, nature, reality, memory, and forgotten engineering exist together.

The player should constantly feel that they are looking at something ancient, beautiful, and impossible.

---

## Visual Pillars

### Pillar One: Ancient but Impossible

Everything should feel old.

Nothing should feel primitive.

Examples:

- Floating pagodas
- Living temples
- Crystal bridges
- Sky gardens
- Singing stone structures

The world is ancient.

The world is not technologically inferior.

---

### Pillar Two: Nature and Civilization are One

The Celestials never separated technology from nature.

Buildings grow.

Roads adapt.

Gardens think.

Cities behave like ecosystems.

Players should rarely know where architecture ends and nature begins.

---

### Pillar Three: Sacred Engineering

Advanced systems should appear spiritual.

Avoid:

- Control panels
- Computer screens
- Keyboards
- Metallic laboratories

Prefer:

- Memory crystals
- Floating glyphs
- Light flowing through stone
- Living murals
- Celestial inscriptions

Technology should feel sacred.

---

## Regional Style

### Dawnfall Village

Warm.

Natural.

Rice terraces.

Wood and stone.

Ancestor shrines.

Mist and rain.

Strong Southeast Asian inspiration.

---

### Spirit Forests

Dreamlike.

Soft light.

Living roots.

Invisible pathways.

Memories appearing within nature.

---

### Naga Domains

Water architecture.

Flowing structures.

Living libraries.

Memory pools.

Ancient archives beneath rivers and oceans.

---

### Dragon Territories

Monumental scale.

Mountains shaped by ancient forces.

Storms trapped inside valleys.

Massive structures built around sleeping dragons.

---

### Celestial Ruins

The most important visual category.

Ruins should not resemble abandoned buildings.

They should resemble forgotten laws of reality.

Players should feel uncertain whether they are exploring architecture, machines, or living systems.

---

## Character Design Philosophy

Every character should reflect the world's themes.

Avoid generic fantasy armor.

Avoid generic robes.

Combine:

- Mythology
- Spiritual symbolism
- Functional engineering
- Ancient aesthetics

Characters should appear as though they belong to a civilization that understands reality differently.

---

## Creature Design Philosophy

Creatures should embody concepts.

Examples:

- Memory Eaters
- Forgotten Ones
- Echo Beasts
- Silent Creatures
- Timeline Guardians

Avoid common fantasy monsters whenever possible.

---

## Color Language

Reality (Dragons)
- Gold
- Deep blue
- Storm silver

Memory (Nagas)
- Emerald
- Turquoise
- Jade

Soul (Spirits)
- White
- Violet
- Moonlight tones

Heaven (Celestials)
- Crystal gold
- Celestial white
- Radiant cyan

The Silence
- Desaturated colors
- Fading light
- Absence rather than darkness

---

## Screenshot Test

A single screenshot should immediately communicate:

This is not a traditional fantasy world.

This is not a futuristic science fiction world.

This is a mythological civilization built upon forgotten reality engineering.

---

## Artistic Goal

The player should feel wonder first.

Curiosity second.

Understanding last.

The world must always appear larger than human knowledge.

Even at the end of the story.
