# Age of Legends: Echoes of Heaven

**Document Type:** Initial Game Concept & Project Plan  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Draft / Pre-production Seed  
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-17  

---

## 1. One-Line Pitch

**Age of Legends: Echoes of Heaven** is an open-world action RPG set in a mythological Southeast Asia where dragons, nagas, spirits, celestial ruins, reincarnation, and time-fractured ancient technology collide.

---

## 2. High Concept

The game begins as a mythological fantasy RPG. The player explores kingdoms, temples, spirit forests, dragon ruins, naga rivers, and cursed battlefields.

Over time, the player discovers that the so-called gods were an ancient civilization whose technology became myth. The player is the reincarnation of the final Celestial Emperor, the ruler who created the Heaven Engine, a machine designed to protect civilization by controlling history.

The central question is not simply whether the player can save the world.

The central question is:

> Should this world continue to exist if it was born from a broken timeline?

---

## 3. Genre

- Primary: Open-world action RPG
- Secondary: Mythological fantasy, ancient civilization mystery, sci-fi time-travel narrative
- Optional future layer: AI-native NPC memory and dynamic quest generation

---

## 4. Target Platforms

Initial planning target:

- PC first
- Console later if successful
- Mobile companion app optional, not core game

Recommended production assumption:

- Build for PC-quality experience first
- Avoid mobile-first scope until the game identity is proven

---

## 5. Core Player Fantasy

The player fantasy is to become a forgotten legendary soul reborn into a world that no longer remembers the truth.

The player should feel:

- Like an explorer uncovering lost divine technology
- Like a warrior between human kingdoms and supernatural realms
- Like a reincarnated ruler confronting past sins
- Like a time-traveler discovering that history has been rewritten
- Like the final judge of whether the current world should be preserved, corrected, or replaced

---

## 6. Creative Pillars

### 6.1 Mythology First

The surface world should feel like myth, not science fiction.

Players first encounter:

- Dragons
- Nagas
- Spirits
- Celestial relics
- Sacred temples
- Prophecies
- Reincarnation

The sci-fi truth should be revealed gradually.

### 6.2 Familiar Gameplay, Unique World

The game should not attempt to innovate every system at once.

The safest direction is:

- Familiar third-person RPG exploration
- Familiar combat and progression
- Unique Southeast Asian mythology-inspired world
- Unique reincarnation and timeline story

### 6.3 Mystery Before Explanation

The world should not immediately explain itself.

Players should ask:

- Why do ruins respond to me?
- Why do dragons recognize me?
- Why do ancient weapons behave like machines?
- Why do memories feel like another life?
- Why are there different versions of history?

### 6.4 Time Travel as Late-Game Power

Time travel should not be available from the start.

Recommended structure:

- Early game: mythological fantasy
- Mid game: ancient civilization mystery
- Late game: limited timeline travel and timeline consequences

### 6.5 Moral Weight

The player should not simply choose good or evil.

The final choices should have heavy consequences:

- Restore the original timeline and erase the current world
- Destroy the Heaven Engine and accept broken history
- Become the new guardian of time

---

## 7. Core Gameplay Loop

Explore → Discover → Fight → Remember → Decide → Change the World

Detailed loop:

1. Explore a region
2. Find ruins, spirits, faction conflicts, or timeline anomalies
3. Fight enemies and solve environmental mysteries
4. Recover memories from a past life
5. Make choices affecting factions and history
6. Unlock new abilities, relics, and regions
7. Eventually influence timeline-level outcomes

## 9. Early Scope Strategy

Do not begin by building the full open world.

The first playable prototype should be a vertical slice.

### Vertical Slice Target

One mythological village near an ancient ruin.

Includes:

- One small village
- One forest path
- One temple ruin
- One mini-boss
- One dragon/naga-related mystery
- One memory flashback
- One moral decision
- One short cinematic reveal that magic may actually be technology

The vertical slice should prove:

- Visual direction
- Exploration feel
- Combat feel
- Story tone
- Reincarnation hook
- Mythology + ancient technology identity

## 10. Suggested Development Philosophy

Start as a narrative and design project, not a technology project.

Recommended order:

1. Story foundation

5. Visual direction

7. Tool selection
8. Market positioning
9. Production roadmap

## 12. Current Decision Record

Chosen direction:

- Asian mythology RPG
- Mythology + lost technology
- Reincarnated hero
- Time travel possible later
- Sci-fi elements hidden beneath ancient myth
- AI NPCs optional, not version 1 foundation

Rejected for now:

- Pure dark fantasy
- Pure sci-fi space RPG
- Fully AI-generated game as the core concept
- Massive AAA scope before a vertical slice
