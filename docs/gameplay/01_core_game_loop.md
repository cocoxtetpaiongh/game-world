# Core Gameplay Loop

## Purpose

This document defines what players spend most of their time doing in Age of Legends: Echoes of Heaven.

The game is an open-world discovery RPG.

Its primary reward is not loot.

Its primary reward is understanding.

---

# Core Loop

Explore.

Discover a mystery.

Investigate its meaning.

Awaken a memory or Timeline Echo.

Gain knowledge, ability, or relationship progress.

Use that new understanding to reach a deeper mystery.

---

# Loop Summary

```text
Explore
↓
Discover Mystery
↓
Investigate
↓
Awaken Memory / Echo
↓
Gain Insight or Ability
↓
Unlock New Path
↓
Discover Bigger Mystery
```

---

# Player Motivation

Players should continue playing because they want to know:

- What happened here?
- Why does this ruin recognize me?
- Who was Aetherion?
- Why are dragons awakening?
- What is the Heaven Engine?
- What is the Silence?
- What kind of future should survive?

Curiosity is the main engine of progression.

---

# Primary Activities

## Exploration

Players move through villages, forests, ruins, mountains, rivers, spirit domains, and Celestial sites.

Exploration should feel deliberate.

The player is not clearing a map.

The player is uncovering layers of reality.

---

## Investigation

Players examine signs, relics, murals, records, memories, local myths, and environmental details.

Investigation connects surface mythology to hidden truth.

Example:

A village shrine appears to be a sacred place.

Closer investigation reveals it sits above a dormant Celestial structure.

---

## Memory Awakening

Important discoveries trigger memory fragments.

These fragments reveal:

- Aetherion's past
- Lost Celestial history
- Alternate versions of events
- Emotional truths hidden from records

Memories are not only cutscenes.

Some may contain playable moments.

---

## Timeline Echoes

Timeline Echoes are short, powerful glimpses into other states of reality.

They may show:

- The Age of Heaven
- A failed timeline
- A modern-like civilization
- A broken future
- A world where a major event happened differently

Timeline Echoes are not full eras.

They are fragments.

Their purpose is mystery, revelation, and emotional impact.

---

## Combat

Combat supports discovery.

It should occur when:

- A place is guarded
- A creature embodies a mystery
- A faction blocks access
- A corrupted Echo must be resolved
- A major emotional conflict escalates

Combat should rarely feel random.

Every major encounter should reveal something about the world.

---

## Companion Interaction

Companions interpret discoveries differently.

A Dragon companion may focus on reality damage.

A Naga companion may focus on lost records.

A Spirit companion may sense soul disturbance.

A Celestial Remnant may recognize hidden systems.

A Human Champion may question whether any of this power should be used.

Companions should help turn discoveries into philosophical conflict.

---

# Reward Types

## Knowledge Rewards

The player learns something meaningful.

Examples:

- A hidden truth about the Fall
- A lost faction history
- A memory of Aetherion
- A clue about the Silence

---

## Ability Rewards

The player gains new ways to interact with the world.

Examples:

- Read ancient inscriptions
- Activate Celestial gates
- Sense Echo Zones
- Communicate with certain spirits
- Stabilize fractured areas

---

## Relationship Rewards

Companions, factions, and major characters react to discoveries.

A choice may deepen trust with one companion while weakening another relationship.

---

## World Access Rewards

Understanding unlocks new paths.

Examples:

- A ruin opens only after learning its memory phrase
- A spirit path appears after resolving a soul conflict
- A Naga archive grants access after proving intent
- A Dragon gate opens only when reality damage is stabilized

---

# Loop Example: Early Game

The player hears a village legend about the Whisperwood.

They enter the forest.

They discover an old shrine.

The shrine reacts to their presence.

A memory of falling Heaven awakens.

The player learns a forgotten phrase.

The phrase opens the Sleeping Gate.

The Sleeping Gate reveals the first Celestial ruin.

The player realizes the village legend was incomplete.

---

# Loop Example: Mid Game

The player explores a Naga river archive.

They discover records of a kingdom that no longer exists.

A Timeline Echo reveals that the kingdom was erased by one of the Heaven Engine's corrections.

A companion argues that the truth should be revealed.

Another argues that revealing it could destabilize the region.

The player chooses what to do with the knowledge.

---

# Loop Example: Late Game

The player reaches a Celestial observatory.

The observatory shows futures where reality becomes perfectly stable and lifeless.

The player realizes the Silence may not be a creature, but the death of possibility itself.

This discovery changes how the final choice is understood.

---

# Design Rules

## Rule 1: Discovery Before Combat

Every major combat encounter should be motivated by discovery, not placed only for difficulty.

---

## Rule 2: Knowledge Unlocks Power

The player should become stronger by understanding the world.

---

## Rule 3: No Empty Ruins

Every major ruin must contain at least one meaningful revelation.

---

## Rule 4: No Generic Quests

Avoid quests that only ask the player to kill enemies or collect objects without narrative meaning.

---

## Rule 5: Mystery Must Escalate

Each discovery should answer one question and create a deeper one.

---

# Scope Guidance

For a first vertical slice, the core loop should be tested in one small region.

Recommended vertical slice loop:

Dawnfall Village.

Whisperwood Forest.

Shrine of the First Rain.

The Sleeping Gate.

One memory awakening.

One meaningful combat encounter.

One companion reaction.

One final revelation.

This is enough to prove the identity of the game without building the full open world.

---

# Core Loop Statement

Age of Legends: Echoes of Heaven is built around the pleasure of uncovering hidden reality beneath myth.

The player explores because they are curious.

They fight because truth is guarded.

They progress because understanding changes what becomes possible.
