# Character Bible

## Purpose

This document defines the major character categories, narrative roles, and design principles for Age of Legends: Echoes of Heaven.

Characters should emerge naturally from the world's themes.

Every major character must connect to at least one of the world's core pillars:

- Reality
- Memory
- Soul
- Heaven
- Time
- Legacy

---

# Character Design Principles

## Principle 1: Everyone Believes They Are Right

No major faction leader or companion should view themselves as evil.

Every important character must have a worldview that makes sense from their perspective.

---

## Principle 2: The Past Shapes the Present

Every major character carries a relationship with history.

Some seek to restore it.

Some seek to escape it.

Some seek revenge for it.

Some seek answers.

---

## Principle 3: Mystery Before Explanation

Characters should reveal themselves gradually.

First impressions should rarely tell the whole truth.

---

# The Protagonist

## Narrative Role

The player character.

The latest reincarnation connected to Emperor Aetherion.

Begins life as an ordinary person living in Dawnfall Village.

Gradually uncovers memories from previous lives and lost timelines.

## Core Theme

Identity.

The protagonist's journey asks:

> Are we defined by who we were, or by who we choose to become?

---

# Companion Structure

## Recommended Core Party

Five primary companions.

Each represents a different perspective on the world.

### Companion Archetypes

1. Dragon Heir
2. Naga Archivist
3. Spirit Wanderer
4. Celestial Remnant
5. Human Champion

Together they represent the major powers of Aetheria.

---

# Dragon Heir

## Role

Representative of the Dragon Dynasty.

## Theme

Duty.

## Core Question

Should reality be protected at any cost?

---

# Naga Archivist

## Role

Keeper of forbidden knowledge.

## Theme

Truth.

## Core Question

Is truth worth the suffering it may cause?

---

# Spirit Wanderer

## Role

Mediator between mortals and spirits.

## Theme

Soul.

## Core Question

What truly survives after death?

---

# Celestial Remnant

## Role

Living connection to the Age of Heaven.

## Theme

Legacy.

## Core Question

Can a broken civilization be redeemed?

---

# Human Champion

## Role

Representative of ordinary humanity.

## Theme

Choice.

## Core Question

Should humanity control its own destiny?

---

# Antagonist Structure

The story should contain multiple layers of opposition.

---

## Layer 1: Regional Antagonists

Bandits.

Corrupt rulers.

Rogue spirits.

Cult leaders.

These create local conflicts.

---

## Layer 2: Faction Antagonists

Leaders whose goals conflict with the player.

They may become allies under certain circumstances.

---

## Layer 3: The Void Cult

The primary ideological opposition.

Believes the current timeline should be destroyed.

---

## Layer 4: The Silence

The ultimate mystery.

Not necessarily evil.

Possibly the final state of reality itself.

Its true nature remains unknown.

---

# Romance Philosophy

Optional.

Character-driven.

No companion exists solely as a romance option.

Relationships should emerge from shared experiences and choices.

---

# Character Progression

Every major companion should evolve throughout the story.

They must challenge their own beliefs.

Their final worldview may differ from where they began.

---

# Narrative Goal

The cast should feel like representatives of competing visions for the future.

When the final decision arrives, the player should understand why different characters support different outcomes.

No ending should satisfy everyone.

And every companion should have something meaningful to lose.
