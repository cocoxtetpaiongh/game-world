# Continents and Regions of Aetheria

## Design Philosophy

The world should feel ancient, mysterious, and layered.

Every region contains:

- Surface Story
- Hidden History
- Celestial Connection
- Timeline Mystery

No location exists purely for scenery.

Every major region should reveal something about Heaven, the Fall, the Silence, Dragons, Nagas, Spirits, or Emperor Aetherion.

---

# The Known World

## Elyrion

The primary continent and the heart of the main story.

Long ago it served as the center of the Celestial Empire.

Today it is divided among kingdoms, spirit territories, dragon domains, and forgotten ruins.

Most of the player's journey takes place here.

---

## The Azure Expanse

A vast ocean stretching beyond known maps.

Contains:

- Naga cities
- Sunken Celestial archives
- Ancient sea routes
- Sleeping sea dragons

Many sailors vanish attempting to cross its deepest waters.

---

## The Crown of Storms

A chain of floating islands suspended high above the world.

Many believe these islands are fragments of Heaven itself.

Ancient Celestial systems remain active here.

Several Dragon Kings are rumored to sleep among the ruins.

---

## The Silent Frontier

A distant land damaged by severe timeline fractures.

Reality behaves unpredictably.

Entire cities appear and disappear.

Different versions of history coexist.

Many scholars believe the first signs of the Silence appeared here.

---

# The Starting Region

## Vale of Mist

The first playable region.

A peaceful valley surrounded by mountains and spirit forests.

Its people live simple lives.

Farmers, hunters, fishermen, traders, and shrine keepers call the valley home.

The player grows up here.

To most people it appears ordinary.

It is not.

---

## Dawnfall Village

The player's home village.

A quiet settlement near the edge of the Whisperwood.

Known for:

- Rice terraces
- River trade
- Ancient shrines
- Seasonal festivals

The story begins here.

---

## Whisperwood Forest

A spirit forest north of Dawnfall.

Locals avoid venturing too deeply.

Travelers sometimes hear voices.

Some return carrying memories that do not belong to them.

Others never return.

---

## Shrine of the First Rain

An ancient shrine hidden within the forest.

The villagers believe it was built by forgotten spirits.

In reality, the shrine sits above a buried Celestial structure.

The player eventually discovers the truth.

---

## The Sleeping Gate

The first major ruin encountered in the story.

Buried beneath Whisperwood Forest.

Sealed for thousands of years.

No one has ever opened it.

When the player approaches, the ruin awakens.

This event begins the central mystery of the game.

---

# Major Future Regions

## Dragonspine Peaks

Mountain territory controlled by ancient dragon lineages.

Contains forgotten vaults and sleeping guardians.

---

## Emerald Rivers

The heartland of the Naga Kingdom.

Many Memory Archives remain hidden here.

---

## Ashen Sky Wastes

The impact zone of a fallen Celestial city.

Fragments of advanced technology remain scattered across the landscape.

---

## The Celestial Crown

A region near the end of the main story.

Contains:

- Timeline Anchors
- Celestial gateways
- Heaven Engine access routes
- The truth of Emperor Aetherion

---

# World Progression

The player journey follows a gradual expansion.

Dawnfall Village.

Whisperwood Forest.

The Sleeping Gate.

Regional kingdoms.

Dragon territories.

Naga realms.

Timeline fractures.

Ruins of Heaven.

The Heaven Engine.

---

# Narrative Progression

Ordinary Life.

Mystery.

Ancient History.

Mythology.

Truth.

Time.

Judgment.

---

# Purpose

The physical world of Aetheria is designed to mirror the player's journey.

Every new region reveals a deeper layer of history.

The farther the player travels from Dawnfall Village, the closer they move toward the truth hidden at the center of Heaven.
