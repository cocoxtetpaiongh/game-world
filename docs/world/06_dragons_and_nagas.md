# Dragons and Nagas

## Overview

Long before the rise of kingdoms and long before the Celestial Empire, two ancient powers shaped the fate of Aetheria.

The Dragons.

The Nagas.

To ordinary people they are legendary creatures.

To historians they are ancient civilizations.

To the Celestials they were allies, rivals, guardians, and witnesses.

Both races survived the Fall of Heaven.

Both remember fragments of the truth.

Neither remembers everything.

---

## The First Covenant

Ancient myths speak of a time before written history.

During this age, Dragons and Nagas formed the First Covenant.

Dragons became guardians of reality.

Nagas became guardians of memory.

Together they maintained balance across the world.

This balance survived for thousands of years.

---

# The Dragons

## Nature

Dragons are beings deeply connected to reality itself.

They instinctively sense disturbances in the world.

They can detect:

- Timeline fractures
- Celestial anomalies
- Echo Zones
- Distortions caused by the Heaven Engine

Many dragons describe reality as something they can hear and feel.

When history is damaged, dragons suffer.

---

## Ancestral Memory

Dragons inherit fragments of memory from previous generations.

Because of this, some dragons still remember the Age of Heaven.

A few remember events from before the Celestials existed.

These memories are incomplete and often symbolic.

---

## Dragon Orders

### Sky Dragons

Guardians of storms and celestial routes.

Many protected floating cities during the Age of Heaven.

### Mountain Dragons

Guardians of deep earth and ancient vaults.

Many Celestial relics remain under their protection.

### Sea Dragons

Masters of oceans and hidden currents.

Their territories overlap with Naga domains.

### Storm Dragons

Enhanced during the Celestial Age.

Many served as protectors of Heaven Engine infrastructure.

### Fractured Dragons

Dragons damaged by timeline instability.

Some possess memories from impossible histories.

Others have gone mad.

---

## Dragon Kings

The greatest dragons are known as Dragon Kings.

Each Dragon King embodies an aspect of reality.

Most disappeared after the Fall of Heaven.

Their awakening is considered a world-changing event.

---

# The Nagas

## Nature

Where dragons protect reality, Nagas protect memory.

They preserve knowledge that would otherwise be lost.

This includes memories from timelines that no longer exist.

The Nagas believe that truth should never be erased.

Even when reality itself changes.

---

## Memory Archives

The greatest Naga achievement is the creation of Memory Archives.

These archives contain:

- Historical records
- Personal memories
- Lost cultures
- Forgotten timelines
- Celestial knowledge

Some archives contain records of people who technically never existed.

---

## River Nagas

Guardians of rivers and inland civilizations.

They frequently interact with humanity.

### Ocean Nagas

Protectors of underwater cities and hidden libraries.

### Deep Nagas

Secretive keepers of the oldest archives.

Many believe they possess records from before the Age of Heaven.

---

# The Great Division

The Fall of Heaven changed everything.

The Dragons believed reality must be protected.

The Nagas believed truth must be preserved.

When timelines began breaking, difficult choices emerged.

Should impossible histories be erased?

Or remembered?

This disagreement created a division that continues into the present age.

---

# Relationship With the Celestials

The Celestials never created Dragons or Nagas.

However, they worked closely with both races.

Dragons became guardians of major Celestial installations.

Nagas became keepers of Celestial archives.

Both races witnessed the rise and fall of Heaven.

---

# Relationship With Aetherion

Neither race agrees on the legacy of Emperor Aetherion.

Some Dragons believe he sacrificed everything to save civilization.

Some Nagas believe his actions caused history itself to suffer.

The truth remains uncertain.

---

# The Reincarnated Emperor

Ancient prophecies among Dragons and Nagas describe the return of a soul linked to Heaven.

A soul carrying memories from many timelines.

Some believe this figure will heal the world.

Others believe they will complete its destruction.

When the player begins awakening forgotten memories, both races take interest.

Their actions will influence the fate of the world.

---

# Narrative Theme

Dragons represent Reality.

Nagas represent Memory.

Their conflict reflects one of the central questions of the story:

> If history and truth come into conflict, which should survive?
