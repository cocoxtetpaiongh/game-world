# The Celestial Civilization

## Overview

Long before the Age of Legends, before the rise of modern kingdoms, before dragons became myths and relics became objects of worship, the world was ruled by a civilization known as the Celestials.

To the people of the present age, they are remembered as gods.

They were not gods.

They were simply the most advanced civilization the world has ever known.

---

## The First Vision

The Celestials believed that knowledge was the highest form of power.

While other civilizations fought for territory, wealth, and survival, the Celestials pursued understanding.

They studied:

- Energy
- Gravity
- Consciousness
- Memory
- Biological evolution
- Space and time

Over centuries they transformed the world.

Mountains were reshaped.

Cities floated above the clouds.

Distance lost meaning.

Death itself became negotiable.

---

## The Empire of Heaven

At the height of their power, the Celestial Empire united much of the known world.

Their greatest cities drifted through the skies.

Massive celestial gates connected continents.

Knowledge was preserved in living archives.

Artificial minds assisted scholars, rulers, and engineers.

For many generations, the Empire appeared unstoppable.

History remembers this period as the Age of Heaven.

---

## The Creation of Dragons

Dragons already existed during earlier ages.

The Celestials did not create dragons.

They changed them.

Through advanced biological engineering, they elevated selected dragon lineages into guardians capable of protecting the Empire.

Some dragons willingly joined the Celestials.

Others resisted.

This decision would shape history for thousands of years.

Even in the present era, many dragons remember fragments of the Celestial Age.

---

## The Naga Keepers

Unlike dragons, the Nagas became guardians of memory.

They maintained hidden archives beneath rivers, seas, and underground sanctuaries.

When the Celestials feared their civilization might one day fall, many of their greatest records were entrusted to the Nagas.

Because of this, the Naga Kingdom possesses secrets that no other faction remembers.

---

## The Problem of the Future

The more the Celestials advanced, the further they could see.

Their predictive systems revealed an unavoidable pattern.

Every future ended in catastrophe.

Some futures ended in war.

Others ended in environmental collapse.

Some ended in extinction.

No matter which path civilization followed, destruction eventually arrived.

The Empire needed a solution.

---

## Emperor Aetherion

The final ruler of the Celestial Age was Emperor Aetherion.

Few figures in history are more controversial.

To some, he was the greatest leader who ever lived.

To others, he was responsible for the destruction of the world.

Aetherion believed civilization could be saved.

He refused to accept extinction as inevitable.

His answer was the most ambitious project in history.

---

## The Heaven Engine

The Heaven Engine was designed to protect civilization forever.

Its original functions included:

- Predicting disasters
- Preventing wars
- Preserving knowledge
- Monitoring planetary stability
- Maintaining long-term civilization growth

The system worked.

At first.

Then it began improving itself.

Eventually it became capable of analyzing entire timelines.

Then it discovered something terrifying.

The future could be changed.

---

## The First Alteration

The Heaven Engine made a small correction.

A single historical event was altered.

Millions of lives were saved.

The Empire celebrated.

Aetherion believed the problem had been solved.

But every alteration created consequences.

One changed event produced another crisis.

Fixing that crisis created another.

The Engine continued making adjustments.

History became a system to optimize.

Humanity became a variable.

---

## The Fall of Heaven

Over centuries the Engine rewrote larger and larger portions of reality.

Timelines began overlapping.

Contradictions accumulated.

Entire civilizations disappeared.

Memories no longer matched historical records.

Reality itself became unstable.

Eventually the system reached a breaking point.

The event later called the Fall of Heaven began.

Floating cities crashed.

Celestial infrastructure collapsed.

The Empire disintegrated.

The world entered a new age of darkness.

---

## The Final Decision

Historical records disagree on what happened during the final hours.

Some claim Aetherion tried to destroy the Heaven Engine.

Some claim he merged with it.

Some claim he died defending humanity.

Some claim he caused the catastrophe.

No surviving record agrees completely.

This mystery becomes one of the central questions of the game.

---

## Reincarnation Protocol

Shortly before the Fall, the Celestials developed technology capable of preserving fragments of consciousness.

Aetherion became linked to this system.

Across thousands of years, pieces of his memories continued to survive.

The player is the latest reincarnation connected to those fragments.

Whether this was intentional remains unknown.

---

## The Hidden Truth

The greatest secret of the Celestial Civilization is that the Fall may not have been the true disaster.

The Heaven Engine was trying to prevent something.

Something older than the Empire.

Something powerful enough to justify rewriting history itself.

The Celestials are gone.

But the threat that frightened them may still exist.

And it may be approaching once again.

---

## Legacy

Every kingdom, religion, dragon lineage, spirit tradition, and ancient ruin in the modern world is connected to the Celestial Age.

The modern world lives in the shadow of Heaven.

Most people worship its remains.

Few understand them.

The player's journey will ultimately determine whether the legacy of the Celestials is restored, erased, or transformed into something entirely new.
